荃灣男浮屍 Tsuen Wan Male Floating Corpse (28)

9月24日早上近11時，警方接報指於荃灣永順街51號海濱位置，發現一具男屍於海面飄浮，救起後證實已身亡，死者為一名28歲姓郭男子。經初步調查，相信事件沒有可疑。

網民得悉事件起疑，猜測死者「被自殺」，包括自殺案前一天曾有網民報料目睹警員現身附近視察，[屍體撈起後] 口鼻有有疑似血水的紅色液體流在地上，雙手一直伸直疑似握緊拳頭、僵硬的向上舉。約有20多名街坊到場了解事件。死者家屬及後亦到場，着現場人士停止拍攝，尊重死者，指他並非涉及反修例運動，不是「被自殺」。消息指死者涉及20多宗假扮官員電話騙案，原定當日到灣仔區域法院應訊。死者在葵芳住所留下兩封遺書，分別給家人及女友。

網民發起「荃灣西海皮獻白花」行動，聲稱要「還手足真相」。晚上7時許，有市民到荃灣西海傍路祭，點蠟燭及獻花。至晚上近九時，現場仍聚集近200名市民來到現場悼念，部分人身穿黑衣，神情哀傷，更有人一度落淚。(Wikipedia)

At nearly 11:00 on the morning of September 24, the police received a report that a male body was found floating on the seashore at No. 51 Wing Shun Street, Tsuen Wan. After the rescue, he was confirmed dead. The deceased was a 28-year-old man named Kwok. After preliminary investigation, it is believed that the incident was not suspicious.

After learning about the incident, Netizens were suspicious and speculated that the deceased was "suicided." One day before the suicide case, a netizen reported that he had witnessed a police officer inspected the nearby area, [after the corpse was picked up] red liquid suspected to be blood flowed from the mouth and nose of the deceased to the ground, and his hands were stiffly stretched upwards like clenched fists. About 20 neighbors came to understand the incident. The family members of the deceased also came to the scene later, asking people to stop shooting and to respect the deceased, saying that he was not involved in anti-ELA Bactivities and was not "killed." It is reported that the deceased was involved in more than 20 phone frauds posing as government officials, and was scheduled to appear in the Wan Chai District Court on the same day. The deceased left two suicide notes at his Kwai Fong's residence, one for his family and one for his girlfriend.

Netizens launched the "Presenting White Flowers at theTsuen Wan West Waterfront" activity, claiming to "return the truth to a brother." At 7 pm, citizens went to the West Coast in Tsuen Wan to have a road-side memorial, lighting candles and presenting flowers. At nearly nine o'clock in the evening, nearly 200 citizens gathered at the scene to mourn. Some of them wore black clothes, their expressions were sad, and some people wept for a time.
